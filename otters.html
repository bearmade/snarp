Learn About Otters

Otters are aquatic mammals that belong to the family Mustelidae, which also includes weasels, badgers, and ferrets. There are 13 species of otters found around the world, and they can be divided into two groups: river otters and sea otters.

River Otters

River otters live near rivers, lakes, and other bodies of freshwater. They have long, streamlined bodies that are perfect for swimming and diving, and they are known for their playful behavior. Some common species of river otters include:

  • American River Otter
  • European Otter
  • Giant Otter
  • Smooth-coated Otter

Sea Otters

Sea otters live in the Pacific Ocean along the coasts of North America and Asia. They are the smallest marine mammals and spend most of their time in the water. They are known for their use of tools, such as using rocks to crack open shellfish. The two species of sea otters are:

  • Northern Sea Otter
  • Southern Sea Otter

Conservation

Many species of otters are endangered or vulnerable due to habitat loss, pollution, hunting, and other factors. It's important to protect these animals and their habitats to ensure their survival for future generations.
